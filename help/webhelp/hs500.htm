Why are measurement results red, green, teal or blue?

Colored measurement results should be used with care.
There are two classes of reasons that lead to red colored results:
  • Missing information on one of the parameters in the calculation, leading to assumptions or estimations for that parameter.
  • Analysis parameters that conflict with the definitions in the Dutch NCS-8 protocol (and probably with most international standards for QA).
    In most cases the related labels are denoted with a bracketed character.

    Missing information or changed information
  • Flatness and symmetry: When one or both penumbra's are missing the field size is undefined. Therefore the In-Field area cannot be determined. Flatness and symmetry can not be calculated. With the Measurement option Accept missing penumbra's the field size is estimated, using extra information from the derivative. 
  • Data labels at horizontal axis: The Measurement option "Invert axis" is chosen for that scan direction. Left and right are exchanged. The sign of the center of field will change.

    Non standard analysis
  • normalisation value, flatness and symmetry in red: The Measurement option Normalise on average of In-Field area is activated for the measurement and/or the reference. According to protocols one single point should be used form normalisation.
  • Depth doses in red: The Measurement option "Use PDD-fit results" and the Setting "PDDfit to buffer" are activated.
  • Flatness and symmetry in blue: The Measurement option Symmetry correction is used. See shifted and symmetry corrected example below.
  • Center and borders in green: The Measurement option Move Profiles to origin is activated. See shifted and symmetry corrected example below.
  • Center and borders in teal: The profile analysis is fallen back to Edge detection (Advanced Settings Tab). The reason is mostly a wedged profile. However highly assymmetric fields might also lead to this situation when the Measurement option normalisation field center is not activated. This may go unnoticed when the Buffer curve is made invisible as in the example below.
